The problem

Launch prices don't stay
where you set them.

Most health systems don't price a new medicine from scratch. They reference the published prices of other countries — each with its own basket, formula, and revision clock. Because those baskets overlap, the world's markets behave as one connected system.

Anchor market sets a visible price → referenced by basket A → which feeds baskets B & C → which re-enter basket A at the next revision

DECISIONS ARE LOCAL —

consequences are global.

Each negotiation is run by one team, against one payer, on one country's terms. But the price they agree enters reference baskets read by many other systems. Nobody at the table owns the network effect.

THE CASCADE IS —

invisible at the table.

The rules are public, but tracing a single concession through overlapping baskets, corridors, medians and second-order loops is beyond any spreadsheet — especially live, mid-negotiation, when the counter-offer is on the table.

IT ARRIVES LATE —

and only moves one way.

Referenced prices update months later, on each country's own cycle. And they ratchet: floors propagate downward through the network, almost never back up. By the time the effect is visible, it's contractual.

Why it matters

Small concessions compound
into structural loss.

Σ

Global value erosion

A single-digit discount in one market can compound, through referencing, into a double-digit share of a product's ten-year global value — quietly, market by market.

⇩

Permanent price floors

Reference baskets remember. Once a low price is publicly visible it becomes the ceiling elsewhere, and it stays in the system through every future revision cycle — for the life of the product.

◔

Distorted launch sequencing

To protect the network, launches in lower-priced markets get delayed or skipped entirely. Commercial defence turns into an access problem: patients wait because of a formula.

?

Negotiating without a computed red line

Payers know the referencing math; most negotiation teams defend positions with judgment alone. Without a walk-away point derived from global value, every concession is a guess.

The ratchet — list price over revision cycles

Each revision re-reads the baskets. Concessions propagate down the steps; nothing in the mechanism carries a price back up.
